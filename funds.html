Class Description

This class explores key concepts in color theory, design software, and studio practice. Students will learn to work with color in Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop and will gain a foundational understanding of the science and psychology of color â€” focusing on Josef Albers Interaction of Color, bauhaus textiles, the California Light and Space movement, as well as contemporary artists.

There will be 3 visual projects throughout the quarter. Work must be clean and shown in a presentable manner. All digital assignments need to be saved as high resolution pdfs and placed in the drop folder before class begins. Students are also required to keep prints and objects from the assignments to demonstrate progress.

Classtime will be used to review student work, conduct software tutorials, and investigate the practical and theoretical dimensions of color.


Grading

Students will be evaluated on the following:

  • 10% project one: hue characterization poster
  • 35% project two: Interaction of Color playing cards
  • 35% project three: Abstract Animations
  • 20% documentation and class participation

Projects – Ability to conceptually and visually respond to assignment prompts and integrate them into compelling objects, technical proficiency, attention to detail, improvement, research, and thoughtful written responses to the readings.

Documentation – Consistent documentation of design processes, highly composed photographic documentation of final objects, organized process book submitted on the last day of class.

Class Participation – Punctuality, focus, articulation of design concepts, and contribution to class discussions.

Classes start at 2:00 PM. If you are 15 minutes late, you will receive a tardy. 3 tardies will turn into 1 absence. Every absence equals 1 full grade down (A to B), 3 unexcused absences result in a failing grade.
