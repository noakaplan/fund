  • Bitcoin

    The first Cryptocurrency and the founding use of blockchain tech, Bitcoin (BTC) is currently the benchmark for stability in the Crypto market. BTC has the highest market cap, along with the highest value of an individual coin, and currently occupies 80% of the market. With a growth rate of over 100% in 2016, we see BTC as an excellent choice for long-term relative stability and potential short term gains.

  • Ethereum

    Ethereum is an open software platform, based on blockchain technology, that allows developers to build and deploy decentralized applications. Ethereum allows for the creation of smart contracts, self-perating computer programs that can facilitate the exchange of money, property, shares, or anything else of value. Because smart contracts run on a blockchain, they will run exactly as programmed, without risk of censorship, downtime, fraud, or thirdparty interference. Ethereum is currently the second most popular currency (by market capitalization) and it supports several up and coming application tokens that can be used to power software deployed on the Ethereum Virtual Machine.

  • Litecoin

    Litecoin is a peer-to-peer decentralized Internet currency that enables instant, near-zero paymets to anyone in the world with no financial intermediary. Litecoin features faster transaction confirmation times and improved storage efficiency over Bitcoin and was created as an alternative. With substantial industry support, trade volume and liquidity, Litecoin is a proven complement to Bitcoin. Litecoin's value has been steadily increasing since its release in 2014 and reached its highest price in early July 2017 at $45.93 per LTC.

  • Iconomi

    Iconomi is a platform for people who are looking for opportunities to get involved in cryptocurrency investing without holding the underlying assets themselves. Iconomi achieves this by allowing users to invest in an index of several cryptocurrencies rather than investing in the individual coins and tokens themselves. Iconomi tokens, known as ICN, allow users to own a portion of the Iconomi fund, giving them voting rights and the ability to receive weekly dividends from the fund. Since its inception, the price of Iconomi reached a max price of $5.38 in late June 2017.

  • Ripple

    Ripple promises secure instant and nearly free global financial transactions of any size with no chargebacks. The Ripple protocol has been increasingly adopted by banks and payments networks such as UniCredit, UBS, and Santander for whom it provices better price and security than Bitcoin. Ripple is the third largest cryptocurrency by market capitalization, only beat by Bitcoin and Ethereum and it has been called an up and coming Bitcoin rival. According to CGAP, Ripple does for payments what SMTP for email, enable systems of different financial institutions to communicate directly.

  • Augur

    Augur is an open-source and decentralized prediction market platform built on Ethereum that allows anyone in the world to create a prediction market on the topic of their choice (e.g. who will win the 2017 world series?). Using this platform, users can buy and sell shares in the outcome of a future event. Holders or REP help to confrim the outcome of this event and can earn further rep by reporting correctly. Augur offers a clean and familiar experience for users, but competition is already brewing and there is a risk they will be pushed out if their platform launch runs into roadblocks. Rep reached its highest price in late June 2017 at $34.50.
